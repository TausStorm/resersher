## Macro Context

The US has imposed a naval blockade on Iranian ports, effectively cutting off ~90% of Iran's seaborne trade. The Strait of Hormuz -- through which ~20% of global oil transits -- is seeing only a handful of ships per day vs. 100+ pre-war. European gas futures jumped 9%+ on the news. Oil prices are elevated and supply is structurally constrained.

**Key dynamics:**
- Iranian oil (~1.5-2.0 mbpd) effectively removed from global supply
- Strait of Hormuz transit severely restricted, rerouting global tanker traffic
- European and Asian gas/LNG prices spiking on supply fears
- US E&P producers positioned as swing suppliers
- Refining margins expanding due to crude supply tightness
- Tanker rates surging due to longer trade routes and supply disruption

## KEY RISKS

1. **Ceasefire/blockade end**: If the US-Iran situation de-escalates quickly, oil prices could drop to $70-75 and tanker rates normalize. This is the #1 risk. **Mitigation**: Use stop-losses aggressively.

2. **Global recession**: A prolonged conflict could tip the global economy into recession, destroying oil demand. **Mitigation**: E&P names with low breakeven costs (FANG, DVN) survive better.

3. **OPEC+ flooding market**: Saudi Arabia could increase production to moderate prices. **Mitigation**: OPEC+ has been disciplined; unlikely to flood market during war.

4. **US production surge**: US shale could ramp up production, moderating prices. **Mitigation**: Takes 6-12 months to bring new wells online; within our timeframe, supply is constrained.

5. **Political/regulatory risk**: Windfall profit taxes, export restrictions. **Mitigation**: Current administration is pro-energy; unlikely in next 6-10 months.
