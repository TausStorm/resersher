# Monitor ERP vs Fortnox: Honest Assessment for UVRelining

## TL;DR

Your frustration is completely valid. Monitor's UX is heavy and unintuitive for someone who just needs to make offers and orders. But switching to Fortnox would be a serious mistake for a manufacturing company. Fortnox cannot do what Monitor does for production. The real problem isn't that you have the wrong system -- it's that Monitor is a production system being forced on a sales team.

---

## What Each System Actually Is

### Monitor ERP

- **Founded:** 1974 (50 years in manufacturing)
- **Purpose:** Full ERP built specifically for manufacturing companies
- **Users:** ~400,000 daily users across 6,000+ companies
- **Market share:** ~50% of Swedish manufacturing companies
- **Architecture:** Windows desktop client (not web-based for core functions)
- **Pricing:** From EUR 93/user/month (cloud)
- **Awards:** Best ERP system in Sweden 2024 (Publikator user survey)

**6 modules:** Manufacturing, Purchase, Sales, Stock, Time Recording, Accounting

The Sales module specifically includes:
- Customer register with CRM
- Quotes -- create, send, monitor, convert to orders
- Customer orders -- register, check delivery times, confirm, monitor
- Delivery process with pick lists and transport management
- Invoicing, accounts receivable, payment reminders
- Sales statistics, delivery reliability, order inflow, cash flow forecasts

### Fortnox

- **Founded:** 2001, based in Vaxjo
- **Purpose:** Web-based accounting and invoicing platform
- **Users:** 500,000+ Swedish companies
- **Architecture:** 100% cloud/web-based, modern UI
- **Pricing:** From ~529 SEK/month for Offert & Order package
- **Strength:** Ease of use, clean design, quick to learn

**Core products:** Accounting, Invoicing, Payroll, Offert & Order, Lager (inventory)

The Offert & Order module includes:
- Create and send quotes via email
- Convert accepted quote to order confirmation
- Status tracking on quotes and orders
- Connect to invoicing for seamless flow
- Batch-invoice multiple orders

## Why Monitor Feels Old-Fashioned (And Why It's Valid)

Your criticism is fair. Here's why Monitor feels the way it does:

1. **Windows desktop client** -- While Monitor has a cloud option (GoCloud), the core interface is a Windows application, not a modern web app. It requires Windows 10/11 Professional, a static IP address, and VPN.

2. **Designed for power users** -- The interface exposes everything because production managers, purchasers, and warehouse workers need deep functionality. A sales person sees the same complexity even though they only use 10% of it.

3. **Not designed for occasional users** -- If you only need to create quotes, the system surface area is overwhelming. You're navigating a cockpit when you just need a steering wheel.

4. **Dense data entry forms** -- Monitor follows traditional ERP UI patterns with many fields, tabs, and sub-windows. Modern web apps have simplified this.

5. **50 years of features** -- Every field and option exists because some manufacturing company needed it. That accumulation creates cognitive overload for simple tasks.

## My Honest Opinion

### Don't switch to Fortnox.

Here's why:

1. **You'd lose manufacturing capability entirely.** Fortnox's "Lager" module has basic structure articles and manufacturing orders, but it's toy-level compared to what a company producing complex machines needs. You'd need to manage production in spreadsheets -- which is going backwards.

2. **Data fragmentation.** If you use Fortnox for sales and something else for production, you lose the single source of truth. When a sales person checks delivery times or stock levels, they'd be looking at disconnected data.

3. **You're part of Sacpro Group now.** Group-level reporting, consolidation, and standardization matter. Running a different ERP than the rest of the group creates friction.

4. **40 users.** At this scale, Fortnox's pricing actually isn't cheaper once you add all the modules you'd need (Offert & Order, Lager, Bokforing, Fakturering, Lon), plus you'd still need a separate production system.

5. **Global expansion.** Monitor operates in 15 countries with multi-currency, multi-language, EDI support. Fortnox is Sweden-focused. As UVRelining expands via APS in the US and through Sacpro globally, Monitor is better positioned.

### What you SHOULD do instead:

**1. Request Monitor sales workflow training**
Contact Monitor and ask for a training session specifically for your sales team, focused only on the quote-to-order-to-invoice workflow. Monitor has a dedicated Academy (e-learning platform) and over 100 in-house consultants. Your team probably only uses a fraction of what's there, and may be navigating inefficiently.

**2. Explore Monitor's web client**
Monitor G5 has a web client alongside the Windows client. The web client is lighter and may be better suited for sales tasks like creating quotes. Ask your Monitor administrator if this is available in your setup.

**3. Explore Monitor Mobile**
For basic operations (checking stock, reviewing orders), Monitor Mobile provides a simpler interface. Useful for sales people who need quick lookups.

**4. Ask Monitor about role-based views**
ERP systems can often be configured to show different views per role. Your admin may be able to simplify the sales team's interface by hiding modules and fields they don't need.

**5. Create process checklists**
If the interface can't be simplified enough, create simple step-by-step guides: "How to create a quote" (5 steps with screenshots), "How to convert quote to order" etc. This reduces the cognitive load of the complex interface.

## Bottom Line

Your frustration with Monitor's UX is the #1 complaint from non-power-users across the manufacturing industry. You're not wrong. But Fortnox and Monitor aren't competitors -- they serve fundamentally different types of companies. Fortnox is for service companies, consultants, e-commerce -- businesses that sell but don't manufacture. Monitor is for companies that make things.

The path forward is making Monitor work better for your sales team, not replacing it with a system that can't support your core business.
