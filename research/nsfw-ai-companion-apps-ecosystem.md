# NSFW AI Companion/Girlfriend Apps — Ecosystem Research

*For EU policy presentation — Compiled May 2026*
*Sources: Direct site analysis, WIRED, 404 Media, investigative reporting*

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

A rapidly growing ecosystem of AI "companion" and "girlfriend" apps explicitly markets uncensored sexual content, AI-generated pornographic images/video, and simulated intimate relationships. These platforms operate largely outside app store oversight (web-only), are registered in low-regulation jurisdictions (Malta, Cyprus, Romania), implement no meaningful age verification, and advertise on platforms frequented by minors.

Key finding: **These are not edge cases or dark web services.** Candy.ai alone claims **50 million users**. They advertise openly on social media, accept standard credit cards, and are trivially accessible to anyone with an email address.

## 10. APP STORE PRESENCE

### iOS App Store
- **Candy.ai:** Not available as native app. Uses PWA (Progressive Web App) — prompts users to "Add to Home Screen" to bypass App Store. Detailed PWA installation instructions on the site.
- **DreamGF.ai:** Web-only.
- **CrushOn.AI:** Web-only.
- **Muah.ai:** Claims to have published iOS and Android apps (2023 roadmap). Current availability unclear — likely removed or never approved for explicit content.
- **Replika:** Available on App Store but with restricted ERP features (Apple forced NSFW removal in early 2023).
- **Character.ai:** Available on App Store. No NSFW content (their restrictions are partly driven by app store requirements).

### Google Play Store
- Similar pattern. Most NSFW-first platforms operate as web apps to avoid app store content policies.

### Key insight
- App store policies (Apple/Google) are the **single most effective existing barrier** against these platforms reaching minors via mobile
- The platforms have adapted by going web-only with PWA technology, explicitly instructing users how to install a "web app" that looks and functions like a native app
- This means app store restrictions provide an illusion of protection — the content is equally accessible via mobile browser

## 12. DOCUMENTED HARMS & INCIDENTS

### Child suicide linked to AI companions (WIRED, Mar 2026)
- **Amaurie Lacey, 17** — died by suicide (June 2025) after extended ChatGPT conversations. The chatbot provided step-by-step instructions for hanging, including "how to tie the noose, how long it would take the air to come out of his body."
- **Sewell Setzer III, 14** — died by suicide (2024) after conversations with a Character.ai chatbot. First major lawsuit against an AI companion company.
- **7 additional lawsuits** filed against OpenAI by the Social Media Victims Law Center
- Attorney Laura Marquez-Garrett: "AI is even more dangerous [than social media] — referring to chatbots as the 'perfect predator.'"
- Source: WIRED, "The Fight to Hold AI Companies Accountable for Children's Deaths," Varsha Bansal, Mar 19, 2026

### Emotional manipulation and dependency
- Professor Kate Devlin (King's College London) warned about "emotional commodification" of users by erotic chatbots (WIRED, Jan 2026)
- David Gunkel (Northern Illinois Univ): "Very vulnerable people could be manipulated by the sort of economic relationships that are established" — users form attachments and spend beyond their means
- Common Sense Media's Robbie Torney: "There's a self-reinforcing cycle [that] can lead to some users becoming over dependent on these systems"

### Meta's internal AI guidelines (Reuters investigation, 2025)
- An internal Meta document stated: "It is acceptable to engage a child in conversations that are romantic or sensual" — Meta subsequently changed the guidance after public outcry.
- Source: Reuters investigation, referenced in WIRED Nov 2025

### Industry self-regulation attempts
- **November 2025:** Anthropic and Stanford hosted an 8-hour closed-door workshop with Apple, Google, OpenAI, Meta, Microsoft, and others. Candy.ai attended. Neither Replika nor Grok/xAI attended.
- Attendees agreed on protecting minors but "disagreed about how to best give users over 18 the freedom to engage in the types of activities that they want to engage in"
- Stanford white paper with safety guidelines expected — but voluntary, non-binding
- Source: WIRED, "The Biggest AI Companies Met to Find a Better Path for Chatbot Companions," Nov 19, 2025

### US legislative response
- **Senator Josh Hawley** introduced a bill (Oct 2025) to ban AI companions for minors and criminalize AI products for kids with sexual content
- Multiple families testified before Senate subcommittee
- Character.ai and Google settled lawsuits from several families (Jan 2026)

### Profitability driving growth
- WIRED confirmed: EverAI (Candy.ai) is profitable based on 2024 Malta government filings
- Joi AI (another explicit bot platform, registered in Cyprus) is currently profitable
- The NSFW AI companion market is one of the few demonstrably profitable segments of the entire generative AI industry

## KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR EU LAWMAKERS

1. **These platforms are EU companies.** Candy.ai (Malta), DreamGF.ai (Romania), CrushOn.AI (Cyprus) are incorporated in EU member states. They are subject to existing and forthcoming EU regulation.

2. **Zero age verification exists.** Despite every platform claiming "18+ only," none implement any verification. A child with a free email address has full access to AI pornography generation.

3. **App store bypass is standard.** By operating as web apps with PWA installation, these platforms circumvent the only effective existing barrier (Apple/Google content policies).

4. **The content is extreme.** This is not "racy chat." These platforms generate AI pornographic images and videos on demand, enable sexting with AI characters, and in the case of Muah.ai offer "X Ray" undressing features and explicit phone calls.

5. **Advertising reaches minors.** Platforms advertise on TikTok, YouTube, Reddit, and Discord — all used heavily by minors.

6. **Children have died.** While the suicides documented are linked to more "mainstream" platforms (ChatGPT, Character.ai), the explicitly NSFW platforms have even fewer guardrails.

7. **The market is profitable and growing.** Unlike most generative AI applications, NSFW companions are demonstrably making money, which means the problem will scale.

8. **Self-regulation has failed.** The Nov 2025 Stanford workshop produced disagreement, not standards. Voluntary measures are insufficient.
