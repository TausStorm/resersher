# US Oil & Gas Investment Thesis: Iran Blockade Scenario

**Date:** April 17, 2026  
**Context:** US-Israel military conflict with Iran (began Feb 28, 2026). Oil prices surged ~70%. Iranian missile strikes damaged Qatar's Ras Laffan LNG complex. Strait of Hormuz shipping disrupted. Oil ~$110+ WTI. Temporary ceasefire talks ongoing but supply disruption continues.

---

## Macro Thesis

Iran exported ~1.5-2M bbl/d (mostly to China) pre-conflict. The US-Iran war has:
- Removed Iranian barrels from market
- Damaged Qatar's LNG infrastructure (world's largest)
- Disrupted Strait of Hormuz shipping
- Pushed WTI oil prices ~70% higher since Feb 28
- Made US energy producers the global swing suppliers

**Key dynamic:** Energy stocks already rallied 20%+ YTD. A Barron's analysis (Apr 4) notes oil stocks may have "priced in" much of the spike. But if the conflict persists or escalates, US producers remain the primary beneficiaries.

## KEY RISKS TO THIS THESIS

1. **Ceasefire / peace deal** — Oil stocks dropped sharply on Apr 8 ceasefire news. If Iran conflict resolves, oil falls and these stocks give back gains.
2. **Already priced in** — Barron's (Apr 4): "Oil stocks look tapped out." Energy sector up 20%+ YTD. Some analysts see limited upside from current levels.
3. **OPEC+ response** — Saudi Arabia and OPEC could increase production to fill the gap, capping oil prices.
4. **Demand destruction** — $110+ oil hurts the global economy. Recession risk could reduce demand.
5. **SPR release** — G-7 could coordinate strategic petroleum reserve releases.
6. **Executive selling** — WSJ reported energy executives sold $1.4B of stock in Q1. Insiders may view current prices as peak.

## KEY UPSIDE CATALYSTS

1. **Escalation** — Strait of Hormuz closure would push oil to $150+
2. **Qatar damage is multi-year** — LNG infrastructure rebuild takes years, not months
3. **Trump admin actively asking producers to boost output** — policy tailwind
4. **US LNG export capacity expanding** — Golden Pass, Corpus Christi expansions coming online
5. **Data center power demand** — structural demand growth for natural gas beyond the Iran thesis
