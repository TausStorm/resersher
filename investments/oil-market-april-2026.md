## 2. THE HORMUZ SITUATION — CRITICAL CONTEXT

### Timeline
1. **Feb 28, 2026:** US + Israel launch war against Iran (ostensibly to prevent nuclear weapon)
2. **Early March:** Iran begins threatening Strait of Hormuz shipping
3. **Mid-March:** Strait effectively closed — oil spikes to highest levels since 2022
4. **Apr 7:** Two-week ceasefire agreed; Iran supposed to open Hormuz; it largely didn't
5. **Apr 12-13:** Failed peace talks in Pakistan (21-hour marathon session, Vance/Kushner/Witkoff vs. Ghalibaf)
6. **Apr 13:** Trump announces US naval blockade of Iran's ports as retaliation for Iran not fully reopening Hormuz
7. **Apr 15:** Iran suspends ALL petrochemical exports (Fars news agency)
8. **Apr 16:** Israel-Lebanon 10-day ceasefire agreed (5pm ET)
9. **Apr 17 (TODAY):** Iran declares Hormuz "completely open" for duration of Lebanon ceasefire — BUT vessels must use Iranian-"coordinated route"
10. **Trump response:** Thanks Iran, BUT says US blockade of Iran ports "REMAINS IN FULL FORCE" until peace deal

### Strait Status RIGHT NOW
- Iran says "completely open" on a coordinated route
- Goldman Sachs analyst Daan Struyven (Apr 15): "Oil flows through Hormuz remain quite low"
- Only "a few commercial vessels" transiting daily during the ceasefire period
- S&P Global's Dan Yergin (Apr 15): "Nightmare scenario for oil is still in play"
- CNBC describes this as "the biggest oil supply disruption in history"
- **~20% of world's crude supplies** passed through Hormuz before the war

### Iran Export Levels
- **Pre-war exports:** Iran was exporting ~1.5-1.8 mb/d (much of it to China via "dark fleet")
- **Current:** Near zero. Iran suspended ALL petrochemical exports (Apr 15). US naval blockade preventing any shipments from Iranian ports
- **Effective reduction:** ~1.5-1.8 mb/d of Iranian oil removed from market
- **Additionally:** Closure of Hormuz affected ~17-20 mb/d of Gulf transit oil (Saudi, UAE, Kuwait, Iraq, Qatar)

- **Round 1 (Apr 12, Pakistan):** 21-hour marathon. Vance, Kushner, Witkoff vs. Iran's Ghalibaf. NO DEAL.
- **Round 2:** Likely next week in Islamabad (per Pakistani officials, Apr 15)
- **Current ceasefire:** Expires Apr 22 (next Tuesday) — US has NOT agreed to extension
- **White House (Apr 15):** "Discussions are being had... we feel good about the prospects of a deal"
- **Trump (Apr 17):** "MOST OF THE POINTS ARE ALREADY NEGOTIATED" — suggests deal close

### Sticking Points
1. Iran nuclear program — US wants complete dismantlement
2. Hezbollah/Lebanon — Israel's campaign vs. Hezbollah was a blocker (now 10-day ceasefire)
3. Hormuz navigation — Iran wants sovereignty over routing; US wants unconditional free passage
4. US blockade of Iran ports — Iran wants lifted immediately
5. Sanctions relief — Iran's core demand

### China Response
- China was Iran's biggest oil customer pre-war (~1+ mb/d via dark fleet)
- US seeking bigger energy foothold in India (CNBC Apr 17) — implies US pushing allies to buy US oil
- India's exports down 7% in March due to Iran war disruption
- South Korea's energy minister: No plans to release oil reserves yet, but pivoting toward renewables
- Iran war sparking "fundamental energy transition" in South Korea

## 6. SCENARIO ANALYSIS

### BULL CASE (Oil goes higher): Brent $120-150
**Trigger:** Ceasefire collapses Apr 22. No peace deal. Blockade + counter-blockade intensify.
- Iran retaliates against US naval blockade
- Hormuz closes completely again
- ~20 mb/d of Gulf oil transit cut off
- SPR releases provide temporary relief but can't fill the gap
- OPEC+ unable to compensate (limited spare capacity ~2-3 mb/d, mostly Saudi/UAE)
- **Price target: Brent $120-150/bbl** — sustained for months
- US gasoline: $6-8/gal
- **Probability: 25-30%**

### BASE CASE (Partial resolution): Brent $85-100
**Trigger:** Messy, drawn-out negotiations. Partial deal reached. Hormuz opens intermittently.
- Iran exports reduced ~50-70% from pre-war levels
- Some Gulf transit resumes but at reduced volume
- Insurance costs for tankers remain sky-high
- OPEC+ holds production steady or modestly increases
- **Price target: Brent $85-100/bbl** — volatile, news-driven
- US gasoline: $3.80-4.50/gal
- **Probability: 45-50%** ← Most likely scenario

### BEAR CASE (Diplomatic resolution): Brent $65-80
**Trigger:** Comprehensive US-Iran deal within 2-3 months. Hormuz fully opens. Blockade lifted.
- Iran resumes ~1.5 mb/d exports
- Gulf transit normalizes
- War risk premium evaporates (~$15-25/bbl currently priced in)
- Iran sanctions partially lifted
- **Price target: Brent $65-80/bbl** — gradual normalization
- US gasoline: $2.80-3.40/gal
- **Probability: 20-25%**

### WILD CARD: Strait of Hormuz Full Escalation — Brent $150-200+
**Trigger:** Military clash in Hormuz. Iran attacks US carrier group or mines the strait. US retaliates with strikes on Iranian coastal defenses. ALL Gulf shipping halted for weeks/months.
- ~20 mb/d taken offline — roughly 20% of global supply
- No OPEC+ spare capacity can compensate
- Global recession triggered by energy shock
- **Price target: Brent $150-200+** — potentially higher in spike
- US gasoline: $8-12/gal
- **Probability: 5-10%** — low but non-zero, especially if ceasefire collapses

## 9. BOTTOM LINE

The oil market is at an inflection point. Today's Hormuz reopening is a temporary de-escalation during a 10-day ceasefire — NOT a resolution. The ceasefire expires Apr 22, and the US blockade of Iran's ports remains. Trump's tone suggests a deal is close, but the Vance-Ghalibaf talks failed last weekend.

**The single most important date is April 22.** If the ceasefire extends and talks resume, oil stays in the $85-95 range. If it collapses, we're back to $100+ fast and possibly $120+.

The 2026 midterms (Nov 3) are the domestic political constraint. At $4.09/gal, 65% of voters blame Trump. Every week of high prices damages Republican prospects. This creates strong White House incentive to cut a deal — even a bad one.
