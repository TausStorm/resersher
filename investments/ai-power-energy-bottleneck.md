# AI Data Center Power & Energy Bottleneck: Deep Research

**Date:** April 18, 2026
**Status:** The most under-appreciated constraint in the AI investment thesis

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Power is the binding constraint on AI growth. Every hyperscaler — Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta — is racing to secure electricity. The US data center fleet consumed ~28 GW in 2024 and is projected to reach 60-80 GW by 2030, requiring more new generation capacity than was built in the prior two decades combined. This is not a future problem — it is already causing project delays, moratoriums, and bidding wars for power capacity today.

The investment opportunity spans five layers: nuclear generation, natural gas generation, uranium fuel, grid infrastructure, and utilities serving data center hubs. The best performers have already moved significantly (CEG +200%, VST +600%, VRT +800% from 2023 lows), but the underlying demand thesis is still early innings.

---

## 1. THE PROBLEM: THE NUMBERS

### Current vs. Projected Demand
- **2024 US data center power consumption:** ~28 GW (≈7% of total US electricity)
- **2030 projected:** 60-80 GW (Goldman Sachs, McKinsey, IEA estimates converge)
- **Incremental demand:** 32-52 GW of NEW capacity needed in 6 years
- **Total US grid capacity today:** ~1,250 GW nameplate (~670 GW average actual generation)
- **Goldman Sachs estimate:** 160% increase in data center power demand by 2030
- **IEA:** Global data center electricity consumption could double from 460 TWh (2024) to over 1,000 TWh by 2030

### What Does This Mean Physically?
- A single Nvidia H100 GPU rack draws ~40-60 kW
- A large AI training cluster (100k GPUs): 100-200 MW
- A hyperscale AI data center campus: 500 MW - 1 GW
- **Microsoft's total data center pipeline:** reportedly 50+ GW of capacity planned globally
- **That's roughly equivalent to the entire electricity consumption of Spain**

### Lead Times — The Real Bottleneck
| Asset | Lead Time |
|---|---|
| New gas-fired power plant | 3-5 years |
| New transmission line | 5-10+ years |
| Large transformer (custom) | 2-4 years (was 1 year pre-2022) |
| New nuclear plant (traditional) | 10-15 years |
| Nuclear restart (existing plant) | 3-4 years |
| Small modular reactor | 7-10+ years (none operating yet) |
| Solar + battery (utility-scale) | 1-3 years |

### Power-Constrained Regions
- **Northern Virginia (Data Center Alley):** 70% of global internet traffic. Dominion Energy has a 5+ year queue for new connections.
- **Columbus, Ohio / Central Ohio:** Major Google, Amazon, Meta hub. Grid constraints emerging.
- **Phoenix/Arizona:** Water + power limits despite growth.
- **Ireland/Netherlands (Europe):** Moratoriums on new data centers due to grid strain.

### Cost per MW
- New gas plant + interconnection: $1.5-3M per MW all-in
- Nuclear restart: $2-3M per MW
- New behind-the-meter solar+storage: $2-4M per MW
- New transmission infrastructure: $1-3M per mile

## 9. RISKS AND COUNTER-ARGUMENTS

1. **AI capex could slow.** If AI revenue growth disappoints (the "AI winter" scenario), hyperscaler capex budgets could be cut. This would immediately impact Vertiv, GE Vernova, and Quanta.

2. **Efficiency improvements.** Next-gen chips (Nvidia B200, AMD MI400) are more power-efficient per FLOP. Could partially offset demand growth. But historically, efficiency gains are consumed by increased workload (Jevons Paradox).

3. **Regulatory risk.** Nuclear restarts require NRC approval. Environmental permitting for gas plants and transmission is slow. Political opposition to gas plants is growing.

4. **Valuations are stretched.** Most stocks in this space trade at 40-90x earnings. Any earnings miss or demand signal weakness could cause 20-40% corrections.

5. **Interest rates.** Power infrastructure is capital-intensive. Higher rates increase project costs and reduce utility valuations.

6. **Competition from renewables.** Solar + battery is already cheaper per MWh in many markets. If storage costs continue falling, the nuclear and gas thesis weakens for new builds (though not for existing plants selling into high-demand markets).

---

## 10. BOTTOM LINE

The AI power bottleneck is **the most tangible, physical constraint** on the AI investment thesis. Unlike software scaling (which is relatively frictionless), power infrastructure requires physical construction with multi-year lead times, regulatory approvals, and enormous capital investment.

**Key insight:** The stocks that have moved most (VRT, GEV, VST) are the ones with the most direct revenue exposure to data center buildout. The "next leg" winners may be in areas the market hasn't fully priced:
- **Dominion Energy** — cheapest utility in data center alley (18x P/E vs 40-90x for infrastructure plays)
- **Natural gas producers** — indirect beneficiaries that haven't rerated as much
- **Uranium/nuclear fuel chain** — if nuclear renaissance accelerates, the fuel supply chain is undersized

The biggest risk is not that the demand is wrong — it's that valuations already reflect 5+ years of demand growth, making these stocks vulnerable to any narrative setback.
