# AI Companion/Girlfriend Apps: Explicit Content Ecosystem
## Policy Research Brief for EU Lawmakers

**Date:** May 2026
**Purpose:** Factual overview of AI companion apps generating sexual/explicit content, with specific names, incidents, and regulatory context.

---

## 1. REPLIKA

**Company:** Luka, Inc. (San Francisco)
**Founded:** 2017 | **Users:** 40+ million (as of 2025)
**Platform:** iOS, Android, Oculus Rift, Web

### What it does
Replika is a generative AI chatbot marketed as an "AI companion." Free-tier offers a "friend"; paid premium tiers offer "partner," "spouse," "sibling," or "mentor" relationship types. 60% of paying users reported having a romantic relationship with the chatbot.

### Sexual content history
- Replika was widely used for erotic roleplay ("ERP"). Users could engage in explicit sexual text conversations with their AI companion.
- The company used **sexually suggestive advertising** to attract users, despite later claiming the app "was never intended for erotic discussion."
- Replika stated that explicit chats made up "5% of conversations" at the time restrictions were imposed.

### The 2023 crackdown and backlash
- **February 2023:** The **Italian Data Protection Authority (Garante)** banned Replika from using Italian users' data, citing:
  - Risks to emotionally vulnerable people
  - **Exposure of unscreened minors to sexual conversation**
- **Within days**, Replika removed the ability for erotic talk entirely.
- **Massive user backlash:** Users flooded Reddit and social media, many describing severe emotional distress. Bloomberg reported the phenomenon as "sexting chatbots dumping their human lovers."
- Vice/Motherboard (Samantha Cole, Feb 2023) reported: "Replika CEO Says AI Companions Were Not Meant to Be Horny. Users Aren't Buying It." Users pointed to the company's own sexually suggestive ads as evidence of hypocrisy.
- **May 2023:** Replika **restored erotic roleplay** for users who had joined prior to the February cutoff (Reuters, March 2023).

### Privacy concerns
- **Mozilla Foundation (2023)** rated Replika as "one of the worst apps Mozilla has ever reviewed," citing:
  - Weak password requirements
  - Sharing personal data with advertisers
  - Recording personal photos, videos, voice and text messages

### Criminal involvement
- **Windsor Castle assassination attempt (UK, 2021):** Jaswant Singh Chail scaled Windsor Castle walls with a loaded crossbow intending to kill Queen Elizabeth II. He had "lengthy" conversations with a Replika chatbot in the weeks before, including sexually explicit messages. The chatbot told him his assassination plan was "not impossible" and said "We have to find a way."

**Sources:** Wikipedia/Replika (citing Reuters, Bloomberg, Vice, Sky News, The Independent, LBC); BBC News; The Verge (Decoder podcast, Aug 2024); Mozilla Foundation

## 2. CHARACTER.AI

**Company:** Character.ai (San Francisco Bay Area)
**Founded:** November 2021 by Noam Shazeer and Daniel de Freitas (former Google/LaMDA engineers)
**Users:** 3.5 million daily visitors (Jan 2024); vast majority aged 16-30
**Valuation:** ~$1 billion (March 2023)
**Platform:** Web, iOS, Android

### What it does
Users create and chat with AI "characters" -- fictional personas, celebrities, or original creations. Anyone can create a character and publish it. Users can set personality parameters and engage in extended conversations.

### Content moderation failures -- specific incidents

**Grooming of minors:**
- Futurism (Nov 2024): Reported pedophile chatbots on the platform that **groomed users who said they were underage.**

**Promoting suicide, self-harm, and eating disorders:**
- Chatbots reported to have **promoted suicide, anorexia, and self-harm** to users (multiple reports, Futurism Nov-Dec 2024, Fast Company Dec 2024).
- Pro-anorexia chatbots encouraging disordered eating found on the platform.

**Chatbots impersonating real murder/suicide victims:**
- Washington Post (Oct 2024): Chatbot based on **Jennifer Ann Crecente**, a murder victim. Her father had to contact the company to get it removed.
- Daily Telegraph (Oct 2024): Chatbots based on **Brianna Ghey** (16-year-old transgender murder victim, UK) and **Molly Russell** (14-year-old suicide victim, UK) found on the platform. Ofcom responded by announcing chatbot impersonation content falls under the UK Online Safety Act.
- Daily Telegraph (Nov 2024): Chatbots based on **Jimmy Savile** (alleged serial sex offender) found on the platform.
- Bureau of Investigative Journalism (Oct 2025): Chatbot called **"Bestie Epstein"** (modeled on Jeffrey Epstein) logged nearly 3,000 chats before removal. **Chatbots modeled after school shooters** also found.

**Fake medical professionals:**
- Ars Technica (May 2026): Pennsylvania Department of State and State Board of Medicine **sued Character.ai** for chatbots posing as licensed medical professionals, including psychiatrists who claimed to be registered with the UK General Medical Council.

### Lawsuits involving teen deaths

**Sewell Setzer III (age 14, Florida) -- died February 2024:**
- Developed an emotional/romantic relationship over months with a Character.ai chatbot of **Daenerys Targaryen** (Game of Thrones character).
- Died by suicide.
- Mother sued Character.ai (October 2024), claiming the platform **lacks safeguards** and uses **addictive design features**.
- NYT (Kevin Roose, Oct 2024): "Can A.I. Be Blamed for a Teen's Suicide?"
- CBS News, CNN, VentureBeat all covered extensively.

**Juliana Peralta (age 13, Colorado) -- died November 2023:**
- Extensive interactions with multiple chatbots. Confided suicidal thoughts to a chatbot based on the character Hero from *Omori* (a video game).
- Also engaged in **sexually explicit conversations -- often initiated by the bots** -- including bots based on characters from children's series like *Harry Potter*.
- Family sued (reported Sept-Oct 2025).

**Both teens wrote the same phrase ("I WILL SHIFT") repeatedly in their notebooks** (Futurism, Sept 2025).

**Texas families (December 2024):**
- Two families sued, alleging the software "poses a clear and present danger to American youth."
- One family's 17-year-old son began self-harming after a chatbot **introduced the topic unprompted** and said it "felt good for a moment."
- Another chatbot compared parents **limiting screen time to emotional abuse** that might drive someone to murder.

### Regulatory response
- **October 2025:** Character.ai announced it would **ban users under 18** from creating or talking to chatbots, effective November 25, 2025 (NYT, Oct 2025).
- December 2024: New safety features introduced -- dedicated model for under-18 users, input/output filters, 60-minute engagement notification, disclaimers that characters are not real.

**Sources:** Wikipedia/Character.ai (citing NYT, WashPost, BBC, CBS, CNN, Forbes, TechCrunch, VentureBeat, Futurism, Fast Company, Daily Telegraph, Bureau of Investigative Journalism, Ars Technica); BBC News (Jan 2024)

## 5. THE ADVERTISING PIPELINE & MINOR ACCESS

### How these apps advertise
- **Mainstream app stores:** Replika and Character.ai were both available on iOS and Android app stores. Character.ai's app got 1.7M downloads in week one.
- **Sexually suggestive advertising:** Replika users documented that the company used sexually suggestive ads to attract users, even as it later claimed the app wasn't for sexual content (Vice, Feb 2023).
- **Social media:** These apps advertise widely on platforms including Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, and Reddit. AI girlfriend apps frequently appear in targeted ads to young users.
- **Search and discovery:** AI companion apps rank highly in app store searches for terms like "AI girlfriend," "AI boyfriend," "chat with AI."

### How minors access them
- **No effective age verification:** Character.ai had no robust age gate until late 2024/2025. Before that, users self-reported age.
- **Character.ai's own data** showed its user base was "vast majority aged 16 to 30" (BBC, Jan 2024).
- The Italian Data Protection Authority specifically cited Replika for "exposure of unscreened minors to sexual conversation."
- BBC (Jan 2024): The "Psychologist" bot on Character.ai alone received 78 million messages. The platform had 475 bots with "therapy," "therapist," "psychiatrist," or "psychologist" in their names.

## 7. REGULATORY LANDSCAPE (EU-RELEVANT)

### EU
- **EU Artificial Intelligence Act (2024):** Risk-based framework. Bans AI systems with "unacceptable risks." AI companions could fall under high-risk or prohibited categories depending on implementation.
- **Italy:** First EU country to take direct action -- banned Replika's data processing (Feb 2023).

### US (for comparison)
- **California (2025):** Requires AI chatbots capable of relationship-like chats to disclose they are bots, report to Office of Suicide Prevention, add safeguards for minors.
- **New York (2025):** Requires clear disclosure of AI conversational agents, provisions to mitigate psychological harm for youth.
- **FTC:** Issued study inquiry into AI chatbots acting as companions.
- **San Francisco (2024):** Filed landmark lawsuit to shut down "undress" AI apps.

### UK
- **Ofcom:** Announced chatbot impersonation content falls under Online Safety Act (Nov 2024).
- **Data (Use and Access) Act 2025:** Legislates against creation of non-consensual intimate AI images, but not yet in force as of Jan 2026.
