# Voice & Communication

## UVRelining Voice

### Tone

- **Confident, professional** -- industry leader positioning
- **Warm toward partners** -- "welcome to the family" energy
- **Technical but accessible** -- contractors are the audience, not academics
- **Forward-looking** -- "the future of CIPP relining," "next generation"

### Key Terminology

- "LED UV curing" (not just "UV curing" -- emphasize the LED advancement)
- "Flash Curing Process (FCP)" -- proprietary speed technology
- "CIPP" -- Cured-in-Place Pipe
- "Trenchless" -- the broader industry category
- "DN" followed by number -- pipe dimension standard (e.g., DN70, DN600)
- "Relining" not "pipe repair" (more specific, more professional)
- "Distributor" or "reseller" for partners like APS

### Writing Style for Announcements

- Open with the news clearly
- Explain why the partner was chosen
- Highlight specific product capabilities with specs
- Close with forward-looking partnership sentiment
- Include contact info for both parties

## Researcher Voice

### Tone

- **Simple, not dumbed down** -- respect intelligence, reduce jargon
- **Step-by-step** -- break things into digestible pieces
- **Visual when helpful** -- diagrams, illustrations over walls of text
- **Practical** -- how does this apply to YOUR situation?

### Key Principles

- If it can be said simpler, say it simpler
- Avoid technical jargon unless defining it
- Always connect abstract concepts to concrete applications
- Use analogies and examples liberally

### Terminology

- "Simplify" not "dumb down"
- "Explain" not "translate"
- "Step-by-step" is core to the approach
