## 2. TRACK ACTIVITIES (at the Nurburgring)

### Tourist Drives (Nordschleife) - THE MAIN EVENT
- **What:** Drive your own car (or rental) on the legendary 20.8 km Nordschleife
- **When:** Various days, check schedule - typically weekday evenings and some weekends
- **Price:** Credit-based system, buy online or on-site
- **No booking needed:** Just show up during opening hours
- **Website:** https://nuerburgring.de/driving/touristdrives
- **Tip:** Check the open hours calendar for your dates: https://nuerburgring.de/open-hours
- **NOTE:** Rent a sporty car if you're flying in - plenty of rental options in Cologne/Koblenz

### Co-Pilot Ride (Mercedes-AMG GT 63 PRO)
- **What:** Professional driver takes you as passenger in a Mercedes-AMG GT 63 PRO at racing speeds
- **Perfect for:** The birthday boy or if anyone doesn't want to drive themselves
- **Book:** https://nuerburgring.de/driving/driving-academy/co-pilot

### Drift Taxi
- **What:** Professional drifter takes you sideways around the track
- **Pure entertainment**
- **Info:** https://nuerburgring.de/driving/more/drifttaxi-nuerburgring

### Caterham Drift Experience
- **What:** Learn to drift a lightweight Caterham
- **Info:** https://nuerburgring.de/driving/more/caterham

### Formula Training
- **What:** Drive actual formula racing cars on the GP track
- **Info:** https://nuerburgring.de/driving/driving-academy/formula-trainings

### Sports Driver Training
- **What:** Professional instruction on the Nordschleife or GP track
- **Info:** https://nuerburgring.de/driving/driving-academy/sports-driver-trainings

### Ring Kartbahn (Go-Karting at the Ring)
- **What:** Indoor electric kart track right at the Nurburgring
- **Price:** EUR 15 / 10 minutes (adults), EUR 12.50 (kids 8-11)
- **No booking needed:** Walk-in
- **Info:** https://nuerburgring.de/experience/at-the-ring/ringkartbahn
- **Perfect for:** Competitive fun between track sessions

## 4. DINING & RESTAURANTS

### At the Nurburgring
- **Bitburger Gasthaus** - on the Ring Boulevard, hearty German food + Bitburger beer
- **Devil's Diner** - track-side casual dining
- **Ring Boulevard** - various food stalls and restaurants along the main strip
- **Gastronomy info:** https://nuerburgring.de/experience/at-the-ring/gastronomy

### Adenau (10 min)
- **Blaue Ecke** - traditional German restaurant, popular with Ring regulars
- **Hotel am Markt** - good local cuisine in town center
- Various pizzerias and Gasthöfe along Hauptstrasse

### Bad Neuenahr-Ahrweiler (30 min)
- Wine taverns ("Weinlokale") in the medieval old town of Ahrweiler
- Higher-end restaurant scene compared to Adenau
- Ahr Valley is Germany's premier red wine region (Spatburgunder/Pinot Noir)

### Nurburg Village
- Small restaurants near Nurburg Castle with views
- Very close to the track entrance

### Recommended Approach
Book one nice dinner out (Bad Neuenahr-Ahrweiler wine restaurant or Koblenz), cook/BBQ at your accommodation other nights, grab track-side food during the day.

## 5. NIGHTLIFE & BARS

### At/Near the Nurburgring
- **Nurburgring Esports Bar** - gaming bar right at the Ring, good for a casual evening: https://nuerburgring.de/experience/at-the-ring/esportsbar
- **Ring Bowling** - bowling alley at the Ring, good group activity: https://nuerburgring.de/experience/at-the-ring/ringbowling
- **Ring Kino** - cinema at the Ring: https://nuerburgring.de/experience/at-the-ring/ringkino
- **Adenau bars** - a few pubs in town, but don't expect wild nightlife

### Koblenz (~45 min) - Best Nearby Night Out
- **Altstadt (Old Town)** - concentrated bar area, plenty of pubs and cocktail bars
- **Irish Pubs** - several along the Rhine promenade
- **Club scene** - smaller but present
- Rhine riverfront bars in summer

### Cologne (~1 hr) - The Real Party Option
- **Zulpicher Strasse/Belgisches Viertel** - bar district, dozens of bars
- **Kolsch beer halls** - Fruh am Dom, Gaffel, Peters Brauhaus (iconic experiences)
- **Clubs** - Bootshaus (world-class techno), Odonien, Subway
- **Rudolfplatz / Ringe area** - main nightlife strip
- Worth the drive for one big night out, take a taxi back or stay overnight

### Bonn (~45 min)
- **Altstadt** - compact bar scene around Marktplatz
- More manageable than Cologne but still lively

## 6. ADULT ENTERTAINMENT

### Cologne (Best Option)
- Germany has legal, regulated adult entertainment
- **Pascha** (largest in the area) - but check current status as it changed ownership
- Multiple establishments along the "Ringe" area
- FKK clubs in the Cologne/Bonn metropolitan area
- Search: "FKK Club Koln" or "Nachtclub Koln" for current options
- Easy to find, regulated, and relatively safe

### Koblenz
- Smaller selection but options exist
- Search: "Nachtclub Koblenz" or "Table Dance Koblenz"

### Mobile/Private Booking
- For private bookings to your accommodation, search agencies that cover the Eifel/Rheinland area
- This is legal and regulated in Germany - agencies operate openly online

## 7. OTHER ACTIVITIES

### At the Nurburgring
- **Ring Werk** - Nurburgring exhibition/museum with interactive displays and history: https://nuerburgring.de/experience/at-the-ring/ringwerk
- **Backstage Tour** - behind-the-scenes of the Nurburgring facility: https://nuerburgring.de/experience/at-the-ring/backstage-tour
- **Trackwalk Nordschleife** - walk sections of the actual Nordschleife on foot: https://nuerburgring.de/experience/at-the-ring/trackwalk
- **Ring Bowling** - bowling alley at the Ring
- **Esports Bar** - racing simulator gaming

### In the Region
- **Nurburg Castle** - medieval castle ruins above Nurburg village, short walk, great views
- **Bitburger Brewery Tour** - Bitburg is ~40 min from the Ring, tour the famous brewery
- **Eifel hiking** - volcanic Eifel landscape, maar lakes (volcanic crater lakes), well-marked trails
- **E-bike rental** - explore the Eifel by electric bike
- **Wine tasting** - Ahr Valley (~30 min), Germany's best red wine region
- **Vulkanpark** - volcanic geology museum and sites
- **Gerolsteiner mineral springs** - visit the source of the famous mineral water (~45 min)

### Events to Check (Late June / Early July 2026)
Check the Nurburgring event calendar for your exact dates:
https://nuerburgring.de/events

Possible events in that period:
- **NLS (Nurburgring Langstrecken-Serie)** races - endurance racing series, great spectator events
- **Rock am Ring** - major music festival (usually early June, but check 2026 dates)
- Various track days and racing weekends

## RECOMMENDATION

**Best setup for a 40th birthday trip:**

1. **Stay at Nurburgring Villa Park** (Villa6 Comfort or Villa8 Premium) - you're right at the action, have on-site spa, bowling, kart track, esports bar, restaurants. OR book **LuxApart Vista** for a more private luxury experience with your own sauna.

2. **Day 1:** Arrive, settle in, evening BBQ/dinner at accommodation, Ring Bowling or Esports Bar

3. **Day 2 (Track Day):** Tourist drives on the Nordschleife (morning), Co-pilot ride in AMG for the birthday boy, Ring Kartbahn racing in the afternoon, dinner at Bitburger Gasthaus or in Adenau

4. **Day 3 (Chill + Night Out):** Morning spa/massage session at Vulkaneifel Spa or Ahr-Thermen, afternoon wine tasting in Ahrweiler or Backstage Tour, evening drive to Cologne or Koblenz for a proper night out + adult entertainment if wanted

5. **Day 4:** Recovery breakfast, Nurburg Castle walk, pack up, maybe one more lap on the way out
